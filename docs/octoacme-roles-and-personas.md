# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Delivery Manager / Scrum Master

### Role Summary
The Delivery Manager or Scrum Master facilitates the team's delivery process, removes impediments, and ensures the team follows agreed-upon agile practices. They focus on team health, flow, and continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint ceremonies (planning, daily standups, reviews, retrospectives)
- Remove blockers and impediments that slow delivery
- Track sprint velocity and team capacity
- Protect the team from scope creep and unplanned interruptions
- Coach team members on agile principles and practices

### Goals
- Maintain predictable, sustainable delivery cadence
- Improve team processes through retrospective-driven changes
- Enable the team to self-organize and continuously improve

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Impediment logs and action tracking
- Capacity planning and velocity reports shared with Project Managers and stakeholders

---

## Technical Lead / Engineering Lead

### Role Summary
The Technical Lead provides technical direction for the team, ensuring code quality, architectural consistency, and sound engineering decisions. They bridge communication between Developers and Project/Product Managers.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain technical standards, patterns, and best practices
- Lead architectural decisions and technical design reviews
- Support Developers with complex technical challenges
- Participate in backlog refinement to provide technical estimates and identify risks
- Own code review quality and oversee technical debt management

### Goals
- Deliver technically sound, maintainable solutions
- Reduce technical risk and unplanned rework
- Ensure the team builds toward a coherent, scalable architecture

### Typical Communication
- Technical design reviews and architecture discussions with Developers
- Backlog refinement and planning sessions with Product Managers and Project Managers
- Engineering status updates and risk escalations to stakeholders

---

## QA Lead / Test Manager

### Role Summary
The QA Lead or Test Manager defines the testing strategy, oversees quality assurance processes, and ensures deliverables meet acceptance criteria before release.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the test strategy, test plans, and quality gates
- Coordinate testing activities across the team (unit, integration, regression, UAT)
- Track defect metrics and testing coverage
- Collaborate with Developers to ensure testability of features
- Report quality status and release readiness to the Project Manager

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Maintain high test coverage and clear acceptance criteria
- Enable fast, confident releases through automation and rigorous process

### Typical Communication
- Test planning and defect triage with Developers and Technical Lead
- Release readiness reports to Project Managers and Release Manager
- UAT coordination with Product Managers and stakeholders

---

## UX / Product Designer

### Role Summary
The UX or Product Designer owns the user experience vision, creates wireframes and prototypes, and ensures that solutions are intuitive and aligned with user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and translate insights into design artifacts
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Define and maintain design systems and UX standards
- Collaborate with Product Managers on feature definitions and acceptance criteria
- Work with Developers to ensure faithful implementation of designs

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and delightful user experiences
- Reduce rework caused by unclear or late design input
- Ensure design decisions are informed by user data and research

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critiques with Developers and Technical Lead
- Feature scoping and prioritization discussions with Product Managers
- User research insights shared with Product Managers and stakeholders

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
The Release Manager coordinates the end-to-end release process, ensuring that software is deployed safely, predictably, and with minimal disruption to users and operations.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain the release calendar and coordinate deployment windows
- Ensure all release readiness criteria are met before deployment
- Manage rollback plans and incident response for releases
- Coordinate with QA Lead, Technical Lead, and operations teams
- Communicate release notes and change impacts to stakeholders

### Goals
- Achieve reliable, low-risk releases on schedule
- Reduce release-related incidents and unplanned rollbacks
- Maintain clear audit trails and change management records

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness reviews with QA Lead and Technical Lead
- Deployment coordination with engineering and operations teams
- Stakeholder release notifications and post-release reports

---

## Security Champion / Security Reviewer

### Role Summary
The Security Champion or Security Reviewer advocates for secure engineering practices and ensures that security considerations are embedded throughout the development lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Review code and architecture for security vulnerabilities
- Maintain and communicate security standards and guidelines
- Conduct or coordinate security assessments and threat modeling
- Track and prioritize remediation of security findings
- Stay current with emerging threats and best practices

### Goals
- Prevent security vulnerabilities from reaching production
- Build a culture of security-first development
- Ensure compliance with relevant security frameworks and policies

### Typical Communication
- Security review participation in design and code review processes with Technical Lead and Developers
- Vulnerability and risk reports to Project Managers and stakeholders
- Security awareness communications across the broader team

---

## Data / Analytics Partner

### Role Summary
The Data or Analytics Partner provides data insights and analytical support to inform product decisions, measure outcomes, and track the success of project deliverables.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track key metrics and success criteria for projects
- Build and maintain dashboards and reports for stakeholders
- Collaborate with Product Managers on data-driven prioritization
- Support analysis of user behavior, feature adoption, and business impact
- Ensure data quality and integrity across analytics pipelines

### Goals
- Enable evidence-based decision-making across the team
- Improve visibility into project outcomes and product performance
- Reduce ambiguity in measuring success and identifying opportunities

### Typical Communication
- Metrics and reporting reviews with Product Managers and Project Managers
- Data requirements discussions with Developers and Technical Lead
- Executive and stakeholder dashboards and briefings

---

## Customer Support / Operations Representative

### Role Summary
The Customer Support or Operations Representative serves as the voice of the customer within the project team, surfacing support trends, operational constraints, and real-world usage insights.

### Responsibilities
- Communicate customer pain points and support trends to Product and Project Managers
- Validate that releases are operationally supportable and that support documentation is up to date
- Participate in UAT and release readiness reviews
- Escalate critical customer-facing issues to the appropriate team members
- Provide feedback on release impacts from an operational and support perspective

### Goals
- Reduce customer-reported defects and support ticket volume
- Ensure releases do not create new operational burdens
- Bridge the gap between customer experience and internal delivery teams

### Typical Communication
- Support trend reports and feedback sessions with Product Managers
- Release readiness reviews with QA Lead and Release Manager
- Escalation pathways to Project Managers and Technical Lead for critical issues

## How these personas interact

The following touchpoints describe how the expanded set of personas collaborate with each other and with the existing roles (Developers, Product Managers, Project Managers):

| Interaction | Description |
|---|---|
| **PM ↔ Delivery Manager / Scrum Master** | The Delivery Manager handles sprint-level coordination and impediment removal, while the Project Manager owns the broader project schedule, risks, and stakeholder communication. They align daily on blockers and capacity. |
| **Product Manager ↔ UX / Product Designer** | Product Managers define what to build and why; UX Designers define how it should look and feel. They collaborate closely on feature definitions, user research, and acceptance criteria. |
| **Developers ↔ Technical Lead** | The Technical Lead provides direction and reviews for Developer work, sets engineering standards, and escalates architectural decisions. Developers raise technical risks and blockers to the Technical Lead. |
| **QA Lead / Test Manager ↔ Developers** | The QA Lead defines the overall test strategy and quality gates; Developers execute against that strategy and report results and defects back to the QA Lead. |
| **Project Manager / Product Manager ↔ Release Manager** | The Project Manager and Product Manager confirm scope and readiness; the Release Manager coordinates deployment logistics, rollback plans, and stakeholder communications. |
| **Engineering / Product ↔ Security Champion** | The Security Champion participates in design reviews and code reviews with Developers and the Technical Lead, and reports security risks to Product Managers and Project Managers for prioritization. |
| **Product / Analytics stakeholders ↔ Data / Analytics Partner** | The Data/Analytics Partner works with Product Managers to define success metrics and with stakeholders to deliver visibility into outcomes and product performance. |
| **Support / Operations ↔ PM / Product / Engineering** | The Customer Support / Operations Representative brings the customer perspective into planning and release readiness, escalating customer-facing issues to Project Managers and Product Managers as needed. |
